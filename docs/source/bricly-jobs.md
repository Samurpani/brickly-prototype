# Bricly Jobs to be Done

## Customer Profile

Mid-to-large off-plan residential developer. Three to five active projects rolling, larger players running multiple blocks simultaneously. Apartment blocks of four to five units minimum, scaling to large multi-block developments. Europe and Dubai. Mid to high-end pricing.

**Sales motion:** Hybrid. Walk-ins direct, the rest outsourced to agencies on 3 to 5%.

**Marketing maturity:** Per-project external agency hiring, sometimes coordinated by one stretched-thin internal marketing person.

---

## Layer 1: Developer-Level Jobs

Six jobs, ranked by deal-close impact. The developer (MD or owner) is the buyer of Bricly. Every developer-level job ladders to one north star: **sell out the development.**

### D1: Capture more buyer demand directly instead of through agencies
*Strongest close driver*

- **Trigger:** Reviewing margin per unit, seeing 3 to 5% paid out on every agency-sourced sale
- **Functional outcome:** Direct-to-developer marketing infrastructure that pulls qualified buyers in without an agency in the middle
- **Constraint:** Without building an in-house marketing department, without losing the agency channel entirely
- **Underlying motivation:** Higher margin per unit on direct sales

### D2: Convert more buyers per render pack by customising the experience to each buyer

- **Trigger:** A buyer is interested but doesn't connect with the standard renders, layout, or finish
- **Functional outcome:** Marketing collateral and renders that adapt to the specific buyer in front of the sales rep, in real time
- **Constraint:** Without re-commissioning the render studio, without architectural compromises that break the constraint model
- **Underlying motivation:** Higher conversion rate on warm leads. Off-plan is hard to sell, personalisation closes the gap.

### D3: Launch a new project to market fast

- **Trigger:** Permits come through, architectural drawings nearing completion
- **Functional outcome:** Project is market-ready (brand, website, renders, brochure, lead capture) in weeks not months
- **Constraint:** Without coordinating five separate agencies, without recurring change-order fees every time the project evolves
- **Underlying motivation:** Get to revenue sooner. Pre-construction sales reduce financing exposure.

### D4: Know whether the sales team is actually working the leads I'm paying for

- **Trigger:** Marketing spend going out, leads coming in, no visibility into agent follow-up, deal stage, or who's performing
- **Functional outcome:** Live visibility into agent activity, lead status, pipeline by source, individual rep performance and commissions earned
- **Constraint:** Without micromanaging, without relying on weekly reports that are already stale by the time they arrive
- **Underlying motivation:** Protect marketing spend, hold the team accountable, surface top performers, replace under-performers

### D5: Forecast sell-out and report to investors and the bank with confidence

- **Trigger:** Board meeting, bank covenant review, investor update due
- **Functional outcome:** Live, defensible numbers on units sold, reserved, projected sell-out date, revenue to date
- **Constraint:** Without spending three days assembling a deck, without data scattered across spreadsheets and agency Dropboxes
- **Underlying motivation:** Credibility with capital, ability to release equity, ability to start the next project

### D6: Use data from past projects to predict and de-risk future ones
*Retention job, lights up at project N+1*

- **Trigger:** Planning the next development, deciding unit mix, pricing tiers, target buyer, marketing budget
- **Functional outcome:** Pattern data from previous projects (buyer types, price points, render performance, channel performance) informs new project strategy before launch
- **Constraint:** Without manually pulling data from old spreadsheets, without losing data when agency relationships end
- **Underlying motivation:** Reduce launch risk, set realistic forecasts, repeat what worked

## Layer 2: Team-Level Jobs

Thirty jobs across four roles. The developer (MD or owner) sits above all four roles as super admin, inheriting the union of all team-level jobs and exercising them based on company size and personal involvement.

### Sales Rep (9 jobs)

#### R1: Work warm leads through to viewing without losing them to follow-up failure

- **Trigger:** Lead in pipeline, last contacted 3 to 7 days ago, not yet at viewing stage
- **Functional outcome:** Rep is prompted who to follow up with, message context pre-loaded, fires off the follow-up from whatever messaging or chat interface the rep already lives in
- **Constraint:** Without opening the CRM, without filling forms, without missing the buyer ready to commit
- **Underlying motivation:** Don't lose the deal to the agency that follows up faster

#### R2: Triage inbound leads from ads and website without wasting time on bad ones

- **Trigger:** New lead drops in from a paid channel
- **Functional outcome:** Lead is auto-qualified or sorted in priority order before the rep sees it
- **Constraint:** Without manually calling 30 unqualified leads to find one buyer
- **Underlying motivation:** Reps work warm pipeline first by default. Cold leads need pre-filtering or they get ignored, wasting marketing spend.

#### R3: Update unit status, holds, reservations, sales conversationally

- **Trigger:** Status changes during or after a buyer meeting
- **Functional outcome:** Rep states the change in natural language through their preferred chat interface (messaging app, email, voice, AI agent), system updates everywhere
- **Constraint:** Without logging into the CRM, without filling forms, without delay between the verbal commitment and system reflection
- **Underlying motivation:** Reps live in conversation, not in software. Admin friction kills compliance. Single source of truth requires zero-friction conversational input.

#### R4: Customise floor plans and renders live during the buyer meeting

- **Trigger:** Mid-meeting, buyer asks for a variant (different finish, layout change, different view)
- **Functional outcome:** Rep generates a customised render or floor plan variant in real time, respecting architectural constraints, hands the buyer something tangible
- **Constraint:** Without breaking the constraint model, without commissioning the render studio, without making the buyer wait
- **Underlying motivation:** Close in the room. A buyer who leaves empty-handed is a two-week chase.

#### R5: Send a personalised follow-up pack after the meeting

- **Trigger:** Meeting ends, buyer interested but not reserved
- **Functional outcome:** Personalised brochure or one-pager with the variants the buyer cared about, lands in the buyer's preferred channel within minutes
- **Constraint:** Without the rep designing it, without sending the same generic PDF every other rep sends
- **Underlying motivation:** Stay differentiated from competing agencies, give the buyer something to show their partner or accountant, control the narrative outside the room

#### R6: Move a buyer from interested to signed promise of sale

- **Trigger:** Buyer commits verbally, ready to reserve
- **Functional outcome:** Reservation captured, hold on the unit, notary appointment booked, deposit terms shared, parties notified
- **Constraint:** Without coordinating five separate threads (manager, ops, notary, buyer, marketing)
- **Underlying motivation:** Lock the deal before the buyer cools or finds a competing project

#### R7: Hand a deal off to ops cleanly once past the rep's stage

- **Trigger:** Promise of sale signed, deal moves to payment plan and contract phase
- **Functional outcome:** Ops picks up with full context, no information lost, rep moves on
- **Constraint:** Without the rep retyping context into another system
- **Underlying motivation:** Track commission, avoid getting pulled back into post-sale admin

#### R8: Track personal commission, expected payouts, and pipeline value

- **Trigger:** Start of week, end of month, before a 1:1, or deciding which deal to push hardest
- **Functional outcome:** Rep sees expected commission per deal, payout timing, gap to target, earnings to date
- **Constraint:** Without manual spreadsheet math, without waiting for manager visibility
- **Underlying motivation:** Commission is the motivation. Visible numbers drive which deals get worked.

#### R9: Match a buyer to the right unit across all live developments

- **Trigger:** Buyer comes in with criteria (budget, timeline, location, lifestyle) but no specific unit
- **Functional outcome:** Rep surfaces matching units across every live development, pivots if first-choice project doesn't fit
- **Constraint:** Without memorising inventory across five projects, without losing the buyer when one project misfires
- **Underlying motivation:** Higher hit rate by working full inventory, not one project

### Sales Manager (8 jobs)

#### M1: Approve unit holds, reservations, and exceptions

- **Trigger:** Rep requests a hold or reservation outside standard terms
- **Functional outcome:** Manager sees the request with full context, approves or rejects, rep and buyer notified, unit status updated
- **Constraint:** Without being a bottleneck that loses deals, without approving things that compromise inventory, without losing requests in chat groups
- **Underlying motivation:** Inventory integrity. Two reps can't hold the same unit for two buyers.

#### M2: Keep inventory and availability accurate at all times

- **Trigger:** Continuously, especially before external pushes (agency shares, marketing campaigns, investor updates)
- **Functional outcome:** One source of truth showing available, held, reserved, sold, with current prices and details
- **Constraint:** Without manually reconciling rep updates from chat threads
- **Underlying motivation:** Every external asset, every agent quote, every developer report depends on this. When it's wrong, deals get double-sold or lost.

#### M3: See which reps are actually working their pipeline

- **Trigger:** Weekly and monthly reviews, lead allocation decisions, performance conversations
- **Functional outcome:** Visibility into rep activity, conversion rates by rep, deals stuck in stage by rep
- **Constraint:** Without micromanaging, without relying on rep self-reports
- **Underlying motivation:** Reps don't self-report honestly. Manager owns knowing who's performing.

#### M4: Verify payouts and commissions before release

- **Trigger:** End of month, deal closes, commission becomes payable
- **Functional outcome:** Manager confirms commission split, referrer payouts, agency commissions are correct, signs off for payment
- **Constraint:** Without recalculating from spreadsheets, without disputes between reps and the company
- **Underlying motivation:** Pay reps right or lose them. Manager is the gatekeeper of correct pay.

#### M5: Keep the team motivated and accountable through visible performance

- **Trigger:** Daily and weekly, especially when morale or numbers dip
- **Functional outcome:** Leaderboards, visible commissions, deal celebrations, top performers surfaced, laggards visible
- **Constraint:** Without manually building leaderboards, without sharing inappropriate detail across the team
- **Underlying motivation:** Sales is psychological. Visible competition and reward drive activity.

#### M6: Brief the developer on pipeline status and projected sell-out

- **Trigger:** Developer check-ins, board prep, financing reviews, ad-hoc requests
- **Functional outcome:** Live pipeline, conversion confidence by stage, projected closes, units committed versus open
- **Constraint:** Without spending half a day building a deck, without stale numbers
- **Underlying motivation:** Manager's credibility depends on answering "where are we?" with defensible numbers instantly.

#### M7: Allocate inbound leads to the right rep

- **Trigger:** New inbound lead arrives, multiple reps could take it
- **Functional outcome:** Lead assigned by capacity, performance, language match, project knowledge, or rotation rules
- **Constraint:** Without favouritism that demotivates the team, without overloading top performers, without leads languishing
- **Underlying motivation:** Lead allocation is a performance tool and a closing tool. Bad allocation kills both.

#### M8: Manage external real estate agency relationships and their deals

- **Trigger:** Agent contacts the manager about a buyer, lead, viewing, or commission claim
- **Functional outcome:** Agency contact logged, lead attributed, viewings tracked, commission verified, fee paid out correctly
- **Constraint:** Without disputes over who sourced whom, without paying on a buyer the developer found directly, without breaking the agent relationship
- **Underlying motivation:** Agencies still drive most volume in the hybrid model. Bad handling loses the channel.

### Marketing Lead (8 jobs)

#### ML1: Brief and coordinate the launch agencies for a new development

- **Trigger:** New project approved for launch, 8 to 12 weeks until go-live
- **Functional outcome:** Render studio, brand agency, web team, ad agency briefed with consistent context, deliverables tracked, revisions managed, launch hits the date
- **Constraint:** Without rebriefing the same context to each agency, without missed handoffs, without deliverables that don't match each other
- **Underlying motivation:** Hit the launch date. Late launch means late sales means delayed cash flow.

#### ML2: Generate the always-on content the project needs week to week

- **Trigger:** Weekly social posting, milestone announcements, price changes, new unit releases, construction progress updates
- **Functional outcome:** On-brand social posts, stories, email updates, web copy generated and published without going back to the agency
- **Constraint:** Without designing it themselves badly, without multi-day agency turnaround, without going off-brand
- **Underlying motivation:** Maintain channel presence between launches. Audiences go cold without consistent content.

#### ML3: Update marketing collateral when project details change

- **Trigger:** Price change, unit sells, payment terms shift, finishes change, completion date moves
- **Functional outcome:** Brochure, website, availability list, ads, social reflect the new reality without each being manually updated
- **Constraint:** Without paying the agency for change orders, without old versions circulating, without buyers seeing inconsistent information
- **Underlying motivation:** Inconsistent information kills credibility. Developer hates paying for revisions.

#### ML4: Equip the sales team with the right collateral for each buyer profile

- **Trigger:** Sales manager or rep needs a brochure, one-pager, deck, or social asset for a specific buyer or buyer type
- **Functional outcome:** On-demand collateral tailored to buyer profile (investor, owner-occupier, expat, first-time buyer), in the right format and language
- **Constraint:** Without designing one-offs per request, without sending generic PDFs
- **Underlying motivation:** Sales conversion depends on the right collateral. Marketing is judged on whether sales hits the number.

#### ML5: Approve and gate-keep what goes out under the project brand

- **Trigger:** Agency delivers a render, brochure, creative, web page, or ad
- **Functional outcome:** Reviewed against brand, project facts, architectural accuracy, regulatory requirements, signed off or sent back
- **Constraint:** Without being a bottleneck, without approving off-brand or factually wrong work, without escalating every decision to the developer
- **Underlying motivation:** Reputation depends on what goes out. Mistakes get blamed on the marketing lead.

#### ML6: Own paid acquisition performance even when execution is outsourced

- **Trigger:** Project at launch, mid-campaign push, units stalling and needing a boost, monthly performance review
- **Functional outcome:** Ads live across Meta, Google, other channels, lead capture working, leads flowing into CRM, performance visible, agency held accountable
- **Constraint:** Without the marketing lead being the media buyer, without losing visibility into agency performance
- **Underlying motivation:** Direct demand is the developer's number-one job. The marketing lead owns delivery against it.

#### ML7: Maintain the project's digital presence (website, listings, search)

- **Trigger:** Continuously, with spikes around launch and milestones
- **Functional outcome:** Project website current, portal listings updated, search basics covered, contact and booking flow working
- **Constraint:** Without rebuilding the website per project, without paying the web agency for every copy edit
- **Underlying motivation:** The website is the conversion endpoint for direct demand. Broken or outdated kills the marketing spend.

#### ML8: Maintain brand consistency between project brands and the parent developer brand

- **Trigger:** New project being branded, agency delivers brand work, social or web going live, collateral being approved
- **Functional outcome:** Each project sits cleanly under the parent developer brand, visual language, tone, quality align across all live projects
- **Constraint:** Without making every project look identical, without manually checking every asset, without different agencies producing inconsistent outputs
- **Underlying motivation:** The parent brand compounds. A buyer who liked Project A should recognise Project B. Brand equity drives repeat buyers and investor confidence.

### Operations / Admin (5 jobs)

#### O1: Prepare and manage the contract paperwork for each deal

- **Trigger:** Deal moves from reservation to promise of sale, then to final contract
- **Functional outcome:** Correct contract template populated with deal-specific details, reviewed, ready for signing, stored against the opportunity
- **Constraint:** Without retyping buyer or unit details from chat threads or email, without using the wrong template version, without contracts living in someone's inbox
- **Underlying motivation:** A missing or wrong contract delays signing. Delays kill deals.

#### O2: Schedule and manage appointments tied to the deal lifecycle

- **Trigger:** Buyer needs a notary appointment, contract signing, payment milestone meeting, handover walkthrough
- **Functional outcome:** Appointment booked with the right parties (buyer, rep, manager, notary, lawyer), confirmed, reminders sent, calendars synced
- **Constraint:** Without back-and-forth across messaging threads, without missed appointments, without the rep losing momentum because of admin delays
- **Underlying motivation:** Every appointment that slips is a deal that cools.

#### O3: Keep deal files organised and complete

- **Trigger:** Deal progresses through stages, each stage requires specific documents (KYC, ID, proof of funds, signed reservation, contracts, payment receipts)
- **Functional outcome:** Every deal has a complete, current file with all required documents in the right place, audit-ready, retrievable on demand
- **Constraint:** Without files scattered across email, drives, and chat, without the rep being asked to find documents they already submitted, without compliance gaps
- **Underlying motivation:** When the bank, lawyer, auditor, or developer asks for a file, ops produces it instantly.

#### O4: Track payment milestones and chase what's overdue

- **Trigger:** Payment plan milestone approaches or passes (deposit, first instalment, mid-construction payments, final balance)
- **Functional outcome:** Overdue or upcoming payments visible, buyer reminded, rep notified, manager alerted if escalation needed, payment confirmed when received
- **Constraint:** Without manual spreadsheet tracking, without the rep being the one chasing, without missed milestones that delay handover
- **Underlying motivation:** Cash flow. Off-plan developments depend on milestone payments coming in on time.

#### O5: Run availability and inventory admin when no sales manager exists

- **Trigger:** Smaller developer with no sales manager, ops absorbs some manager work
- **Functional outcome:** Availability list accurate, holds and reservations logged correctly, basic inventory hygiene maintained
- **Constraint:** Without ops stepping into sales decisions they're not authorised to make
- **Underlying motivation:** Inventory still needs an owner even when there's no manager.

## Layer 3: Agent-Level Jobs

Eleven jobs, tagged by build phase. The agent layer is the architectural promise of Bricly's AI-native and MCP-native foundations. Most are roadmap, not MVP.

### Type A: Internal Agents (Bricly's own AI services)

#### A1: Conversational rep input agent
*Phase: MVP*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Sales rep
- **Trigger:** Rep messages or speaks an update through their preferred chat or voice interface (status change, hold request, follow-up note, lead captured at a meeting)
- **Functional outcome:** Agent parses the message, updates the right CRM records (unit status, opportunity stage, contact note, calendar entry), confirms back to the rep, escalates approval requests to the manager
- **Constraint:** Must handle ambiguity (which unit, which buyer), must not act on changes that need approval, must confirm before executing destructive actions
- **Underlying motivation:** Reps don't use CRMs. The CRM stays current only if input is conversational and frictionless.

#### A2: Lead qualification and triage agent
*Phase: Phase 2*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Sales manager and rep
- **Trigger:** New inbound lead arrives from any channel
- **Functional outcome:** Agent enriches the lead, scores it against project fit and buyer profile, assigns to the right rep based on rules, drafts an initial outreach message in the rep's voice
- **Constraint:** Must not auto-send without rep approval, must defer to manager rules for allocation, must flag low-confidence enrichment
- **Underlying motivation:** Cold leads get ignored by reps. Agent triage protects marketing spend.

#### A3: Render and collateral generation agent
*Phase: MVP for in-meeting render variants. Phase 2 for autonomous campaign generation.*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Sales rep, marketing lead
- **Trigger:** Rep requests a variant during a buyer meeting, or marketing lead requests a campaign asset, or a project detail changes that requires collateral updates
- **Functional outcome:** Agent generates the requested asset using Midjourney, Canva, and the constraint model, respecting architectural and brand limits
- **Constraint:** Must respect the JSON constraint model, must stay on-brand, must not hallucinate units or features that don't exist
- **Underlying motivation:** Personalisation at the speed of the conversation.

#### A4: Follow-up and nurture agent
*Phase: Phase 2*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Sales rep
- **Trigger:** Lead has gone X days without contact, milestone in the buyer journey hits, anniversary of last meeting, project milestone the buyer cares about
- **Functional outcome:** Agent drafts a personalised follow-up message referencing the buyer's stated interests and the unit they viewed, queues it for rep approval or sends if rep has pre-authorised, logs the activity
- **Constraint:** Must not feel templated, must pause if buyer has reserved or gone cold by request
- **Underlying motivation:** Follow-up failure is where deals die. Reps will not do this manually at volume.

#### A5: Inventory and collateral sync agent
*Phase: MVP for internal CRM-Studio sync. Phase 2 for full external propagation.*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Marketing lead, sales manager
- **Trigger:** Unit status changes, price changes, payment terms change, new unit released
- **Functional outcome:** Agent propagates the change across every piece of collateral (brochure, website, availability PDF, social, ads, agent shares), versions updated, stale versions retired
- **Constraint:** Must not push to public channels without approval rules, must keep an audit trail
- **Underlying motivation:** Inventory drift is the manager's nightmare. Manual sync across collateral is what marketing can't keep up with.

#### A6: Reporting and forecasting agent
*Phase: Phase 2*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Developer, sales manager
- **Trigger:** Scheduled (weekly, monthly) or on-demand from the developer or manager
- **Functional outcome:** Agent generates a live pipeline report, sell-out forecast, marketing ROI breakdown, agent and channel performance summary, in the format the recipient wants
- **Constraint:** Must use live data, must caveat confidence levels, must not invent numbers when data is missing
- **Underlying motivation:** Instant defensible numbers. Manual reporting is a half-day job that's stale on arrival.

#### A7: Cross-project pattern agent
*Phase: Later, requires multi-project data*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Developer
- **Trigger:** New project being planned, pricing decision, marketing budget allocation, unit mix question
- **Functional outcome:** Agent surfaces patterns from completed and live projects and gives an evidence-based recommendation
- **Constraint:** Requires multi-project history to be useful
- **Underlying motivation:** Reduce launch risk through evidence. Compounds as the developer runs more projects on Bricly.

#### A11: Inbound lead conversational qualification and appointment setting
*Phase: Phase 2*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Sales rep, sales manager
- **Trigger:** New inbound lead arrives from any channel
- **Functional outcome:** Agent opens a conversation with the lead through their preferred channel, asks qualifying questions, surfaces matching units, offers viewing slots based on rep availability, books the appointment, hands the qualified lead to the assigned rep with the conversation history attached
- **Constraint:** Must defer to manager rules for allocation, must not misrepresent units or prices, must escalate to a human if confidence drops, must respect the buyer's right to be handed to a human at any point
- **Underlying motivation:** Reps work warm pipeline by default and ignore cold leads. An agent that runs the cold-to-warm conversion stage without rep effort makes paid acquisition viable. Rep walks into a meeting with a pre-qualified, briefed buyer.

#### A12: On-demand consultant and decision-support agent
*Phase: MVP for project launch and brand consultation. Phase 2 for broader decision support (pricing, channel mix, budget allocation, performance review, cross-project pattern review).*

- **Acts on behalf of:** Developer, marketing lead, sales manager, C-level
- **Trigger:** Two patterns. (1) Workflow-embedded: after project intake (CRM project wizard: name, developer, type, status, completion, price range, location, units, amenities, target buyer, availability list, files) and Studio brief intake (style direction, brand stage, timeline, references) are complete and ConstraintModel extraction has produced a Pending_Review version, the agent opens a project_launch consultation before creative generation runs. (2) On-demand: any User can summon the consultant for any decision (pricing, lead allocation, campaign direction, channel mix, budget allocation, performance review, cross-project pattern review).
- **Functional outcome:** Agent loads the relevant context for the consultation type (full project intake + extracted constraints + corporate brand + prior-project patterns for project_launch; pipeline state and performance metrics for performance_review; campaign attribution and channel data for budget_allocation; etc.) and runs a focused, adaptive Q&A within a soft cap to validate the user's thinking, surface gaps, share comparable data and references, and recommend direction. Concludes by capturing locked decisions and producing downstream outputs (Briefs, price updates, campaign changes, assignment rule changes, depending on type). Reusable across decision types via a type discriminator on the ConsultationSession entity.
- **Constraint:** Must stay within the soft cap unless the user opts in to more depth, must surface (not invent) references with sources, must respect data permissions (manager can't see other managers' deals; reps can't see C-level reports), must persist the reasoning as a ConsultationSession for audit and pattern learning, must produce structured outputs (not free-text summaries), must escalate to a human when confidence is low
- **Underlying motivation:** This is the most visible expression of the AI-as-OS principle. At launch, it's the difference between "AI generated four logos, none felt right" and "AI understood the project and gave me the right four." Outside the launch flow, it's the difference between a marketing lead Googling "how to allocate paid budget across channels" and a marketing lead getting an evidence-based recommendation from their own data in 5 minutes. Feeds D6 (retention) by compounding decision history across projects. The same agent capability serves operational decisions, strategic decisions, and creative decisions through one entity with a type discriminator.

### Type B: External Agents (clients of the Bricly MCP server and API)

#### A8: Buyer's personal AI assistant accessing project data
*Phase: Later*

- **Acts on behalf of:** The buyer
- **Trigger:** Buyer asks their assistant to find off-plan units matching criteria, get availability, request a viewing
- **Functional outcome:** Buyer's agent connects via Bricly's MCP server, reads project resources, can request a viewing or generate a personalised brochure
- **Constraint:** Must respect rate limits, must not expose private CRM data, must route enquiries to the right rep
- **Underlying motivation:** Bricly becomes a data source for the AI agents buyers will use. Direct demand at scale.

#### A9: Real estate agency AI submitting leads and tracking commissions
*Phase: Phase 2 or later*

- **Acts on behalf of:** External real estate agencies
- **Trigger:** Agency wants to submit a buyer interest, request availability for a client, claim attribution on a closed deal
- **Functional outcome:** Agency's agent connects via MCP, submits the lead with attribution, tracks the deal status, sees commission accrual
- **Constraint:** Must enforce attribution rules, must not give agencies access to other agencies' deals, must hold up to commission disputes
- **Underlying motivation:** Agencies are 60 to 80% of volume. Programmatic agency integration is a moat.

#### A10: Developer's own automations and integrations
*Phase: Phase 2*

- **Acts on behalf of:** The developer's super admin or marketing lead
- **Trigger:** Developer wants to wire Bricly into their own stack
- **Functional outcome:** Developer connects their tools to Bricly via API or MCP, builds custom workflows
- **Constraint:** Must respect Bricly's data model, must use the same auth and permissions as humans
- **Underlying motivation:** Bricly becomes the system of record other developer systems consume. Customer lock-in by integration depth.

### MVP scope at agent level

- A1: Conversational rep input agent
- A3 (partial): In-meeting render variant generation
- A5 (partial): Internal CRM-Studio sync
- A12 (partial): On-demand consultant — project launch and brand consultation only at MVP; broader decision support (pricing, channel mix, performance review, cross-project) is Phase 2

### Strategic notes

1. **D1 is the headline job.** Sell direct, capture more demand, replace the agency margin. Every demo, every landing page, every sales call leads with this.
2. **D6 and A7 are retention jobs.** They depend on multi-project data that doesn't exist on day one. Don't sell on them. They become the reason customers don't churn.
3. **CRM-side complexity is the time sink.** Studio is more demoable, CRM is where the operational depth lives (commissions, agency attribution, payment milestones, document storage).
4. **A11 is the most strategically interesting Phase 2 job.** Buyer-facing agent on the developer's behalf. Accuracy matters more than speed. A bad A11 conversation kills a deal and hurts the developer's brand.
5. **A12 is the visible AI-OS moment, and it scales beyond launch.** At project launch it's the step where Bricly stops feeling like a tool and starts feeling like a teammate. Project intake feeds in, agent comes back with informed direction, brand generation respects that direction. The same consultant pattern is available on-demand for any decision: pricing, channel mix, lead allocation, performance review, strategic cross-project review. One entity, one capability, expressed differently by type. This is what AI-as-the-OS looks like in product form.
