# **Bricly Marketing Collateral Production Standard (end to end)**

This documents every piece of marketing collateral produced for an off-plan project, and how to produce it correctly, in order, from brand through to website. It is a craft and training standard. The rest of the library documents what the system does and how the agents orchestrate it. This document is the opposite cut: it ignores the orchestration layer and describes the work itself, what to make, how to make it, and the standard it must hit, so it can train the generation system and the team.  
Suggested code: MKT-CP-01, a master cross-cutting standard that sits above the per-subcategory SOPs (Brand, Renders, Ads, Social, Launch, Website). Reposition if you prefer.

**How to use this**

* Read top to bottom. The stages are in production order.  
* Each stage lists what gets produced, what it needs first, the process to produce it, and the standard.  
* The shaded blocks are where your expert input goes: the tools and creators to use, how to prepare and upload source images and creatives, the step-by-step to get it right, and worked examples. Fill them in as you lock each one.  
* Honour the stage gates. Do not start a stage before its inputs exist.

# **Production sequence at a glance**

Start asset development 1 to 2 months before launch. Produce in this order, because each stage inherits from the one before it.  
1\. Brand. The identity everything else inherits. Nothing visual is produced before this exists.

2\. Renders. The visual product. Before the building exists, the render is the product.

3\. Ad creative. Brand plus renders become property-specific paid creative. Full standard in MKT-AD-14.

4\. Social. The organic presence that supports and sustains the launch, split by project tier.

5\. Launch campaign. The timed collateral and sequence that drives the launch moment, run over a four-week pre-launch window.

6\. Website. The destination that sells the vision and hosts the gated price list and registration. Everything funnels here.

**Stage gates**

* No renders before CAD, the constraint model and the brand kit exist.  
* No ad or social creative before approved renders and an Active brand kit.  
* No launch sequence before the ad, social and website assets are ready.  
* No public website launch before availability and pricing are synced and the reserve mechanic is tested.

**Types of launches**

**High-End Luxury ($1m+, mix developments)-** Should be done with highest quality \- branding, renders should be custom/human to achieve highest standard  

**Mid-Sized Project (20-50 units) \-** Should be a hybrid of both

**Small Project \-** Quicker turn around \- most can be automated

# **Stage 1\. Brand and identity**

**Produces:** Project name and naming system, logo and variants, colour palette, typography, brand kit and guidelines, buyer personas, tone of voice, core messaging.  
**Needs first:** Developer positioning and target buyer, location and market context, the architect's project facts.  
**Feeds into:** Every downstream stage. The brand kit is the single source all collateral pulls from.

**What gets produced**

* Project name and a naming system for buildings, phases and units.  
* Logo, variants (full, mark, mono, reversed) and clear-space rules.  
* Colour palette and typography with usage rules.  
* Brand kit and guidelines, stored as the single shared source.  
* Buyer personas (who buys, what they want, what moves them).  
* Tone of voice and the core messaging or value anchor.

**Process**

1\. Run the brand consultation: positioning, audience, references, what the project is and is not.

2\. Define the personas. These drive copy and targeting downstream.

3\. Define the naming system so units and phases name consistently across every surface.

4\. Develop logo concepts, select one, refine, produce variants and clear-space rules.

5\. Set the palette and type with explicit usage rules, including how they sit over renders.

6\. Write the tone of voice and the core messaging.

7\. Assemble the brand kit so every downstream producer pulls from one place.

**Standard**

* Consistent on every surface, legible at small sizes, and readable as an overlay on renders.  
* Distinct from local competitors.  
* Personas are specific enough to write copy and set targeting from.

# **Stage 2\. Renders and visual assets**

**Produces:** Exterior renders, interior renders per unit type, amenity renders, aerial and location context, styled floor plans, unit-type visuals, lifestyle imagery.  
**Needs first:** Architect CAD and drawings, the constraint model (slope, setbacks, height, materials, location, style), the brand kit.  
**Feeds into:** Ad creative, social, launch collateral and the website. This is the visual spine of everything.

**What gets produced**

* Hero exterior renders.  
* Interior renders for each sellable unit type.  
* Amenity renders (pool, gym, lobby, shared spaces).  
* Aerial and location-context views.  
* Styled floor plans and unit-type visuals.  
* Lifestyle imagery for higher-funnel and international use.  
* A complete, tagged render set per sellable type.

**Process**

1\. Gather the CAD and drawings, confirm they are complete and current.

2\. Build or confirm the constraint model, so every render respects the real architecture.

3\. Define the shot list per type: hero exterior, key interiors, amenities, the view.

4\. Set the visual style: time of day, mood, materials, staging, on-brand.

5\. Generate the renders.

6\. Verify each render against the constraint model and reality: proportions, materials, context, light.

7\. Retouch and finalise.

8\. Approve and file into the asset library, tagged by type and use.

**Standard**

* Architecturally accurate to the constraint model. No invented geometry, materials or context.  
* On-brand and consistent in style across the whole set.  
* High enough quality to sell the unit before it exists.  
* Carries the render disclaimer wherever shown.

# **Stage 4\. Website and microsite**

**Produces:** Public microsite, registration gate, gated price list with two availability views, countdown, reserve and holding-fee pay gate, lead tracking, SEO, agent portal.  
**Needs first:** Brand, renders, copy, availability and pricing from the CRM, all collateral above.  
**Feeds into:** The destination everything funnels into. The point of sale for the launch.

**What gets produced**

* The public microsite: hero, apartment types and details, amenities, location, call to action.  
* The registration gate that captures the lead and unlocks browsing.  
* The gated price list with two availability views: a visual where buyers hover over the building to see each apartment, and a grid or list view, both with filters.  
* A countdown to launch day.  
* The reserve mechanic with a 500-euro refundable holding-fee pay gate, on a first-come basis at launch.  
* Lead tracking so registration fires a tracked lead for every ad platform.  
* SEO and metadata.  
* An agent portal, separate from the microsite, where agents see all available properties.

**Process**

1\. Build the content backbone: copy and assets for each section.

2\. Build the public microsite that sells the vision and ends on a single browse-availability call to action.

3\. Build the registration gate. On submit, create the lead and grant browse access.

4\. Build the gated availability: the visual hover view and the grid or list view, both filterable.

5\. Add the countdown. Keep reserve locked pre-launch, browse and shortlist only.

6\. Wire the reserve and the 500-euro refundable holding-fee pay gate for launch day.

7\. Set lead tracking and pixels so registration fires a tracked lead on submission.

8\. Set SEO and metadata.

9\. Stand up the agent portal, separate from the buyer microsite.

10\. Sync availability and pricing live from the CRM, so sold and held-back units drop everywhere in real time.

**Standard**

* Vision first on the public page. Prices and unit detail only behind the gate (always recommended, developer-toggleable).  
* Real-time availability, atomic reserve, no double-claims.  
* Fast, on-brand, and every registration tracked.

The microsite build and publish, the gate and the reserve transaction are documented in MKT-WB-03, MKT-WB-07 and SAL-PP-01. This stage is the collateral and content that fill them.

# **Stage 3\. Ad creative**

**Produces:** Unit-type ad sets (e.g. penthouse), project-level ad sets (amenities, starting price), per format and persona, plus international video creative where needed.  
**Needs first:** Brand kit, approved renders, personas, starting prices, the campaign brief.  
**Feeds into:** The launch campaign and the website (every ad drives to the registration gate).

**What gets produced**

* Unit-type ad sets, one per sellable type, starting from that type's render.  
* Project-level ad sets: amenities, location, starting price, in the same order of action.  
* Each in the required formats (1:1, 4:5, 9:16, 16:9), per persona.  
* International video creative, lifestyle-led, as a separate set.

**Process (summary)**

1\. Select the ad type for the objective: unit-type or project-level.

2\. Pull the render, brand, key details and starting price.

3\. Write copy to the persona and the fixed ad anatomy.

4\. Produce the image or video, apply styling, render each format.

5\. Set the destination to the registration gate, not a platform lead form.

6\. QA against the standard and file.

**Standard**

* Property-specific, never a generic catch-all.  
* On-brand and to the fixed ad anatomy.  
* Destination is the registration gate, tracked as a lead on submission.

The full ad craft standard, including the image, video, styling and winning-example fill-in blocks, lives in MKT-AD-14. Complete those there rather than duplicating them here. This stage is the summary so the end-to-end sequence reads in one place.

# **Stage 4\. Social (organic)**

**Produces:** Social pages set up on-brand, organic content per type and platform at correct specs, a posting calendar, split by project tier.  
**Needs first:** Brand kit, renders, video, the launch timeline.  
**Feeds into:** Top-of-funnel awareness, launch support, and sustained interest after launch.

**What gets produced**

* The social pages and profiles for the project, on-brand, at correct profile and cover sizes.  
* Content per type: renders, amenity videos, walkthroughs, behind-the-scenes, countdown posts, social proof.  
* Each asset at the correct resolution and aspect ratio per platform, with safe zones respected.  
* A posting calendar timed to the launch sequence: teaser, countdown, launch, sustained.

**Process**

1\. Decide which pages and platforms to create for this project, by tier (luxury, medium, smaller).

2\. Set up the profiles on-brand: handles, bios, profile and cover assets at the right sizes.

3\. Define the content plan per tier: post types, mix and cadence.

4\. Produce posts from renders and video to platform specs.

5\. Schedule against the launch timeline.

6\. Community-manage: respond, route enquiries to registration.

**Standard**

* On-brand and consistent with the paid creative.  
* Correct specs per platform, no stretched or wrongly-cropped assets.  
* Cadence and effort matched to the project tier.

# **Stage 5\. Launch campaign (collateral and sequence)**

**Produces:** Teaser and registration-drive assets, countdown creative, waitlist and early-access comms, launch-day asset set, email sequences, reminders, post-launch sustained creative.  
**Needs first:** Brand, renders, ad creative, social, the launch-model decision, the four-week pre-launch window.  
**Feeds into:** The launch moment and the selling period after it.

**What gets produced**

* Pre-launch teaser and registration-drive assets across paid and social.  
* Countdown creative for every channel.  
* Waitlist and early-access communications.  
* The launch-day asset set: announcements, reserve-is-open creative, social, emails.  
* Email sequences: developer-list blast, registrant nurture, launch-day notifications.  
* Reminder notifications so registrants are alerted that reserve is open on launch day.  
* Post-launch sustained creative to replace fall-off through the selling period.

**Process**

1\. Choose the launch model with the developer: build-up-and-launch for investor stock, or straight-to-market for end-user products.

2\. Build the pre-launch teaser and registration drive for the four-week window.

3\. Produce countdown collateral across channels.

4\. Prepare the waitlist and early-access comms.

5\. Produce the launch-day asset set and the email blasts.

6\. Set the registrant reminders and launch-day reserve alerts.

7\. Prepare post-launch sustained creative, so spend is never cut to mine the database.

**Standard**

* Consistent across every channel and timed to the sequence.  
* Every asset ready 1 to 2 months ahead, not built on launch week.  
* The four-week pre-launch window and the launch-day option are developer toggles.

The launch strategy and the launch-day runbook are documented in MKT-LN-01 and MKT-LN-02. This stage is the collateral and comms that those orchestrate.
